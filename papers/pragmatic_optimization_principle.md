# Pragmatic Optimization Principle

The objective of 5DMarkets is not perfect simulation of all market participants.

Financial markets consist of heterogeneous agents whose internal decision processes are shaped by individual experience, prior success, behavioral bias, and subjective belief systems.

These internal decision processes cannot be fully modeled.

------------------------------------------------------------
AGENT PATH DEPENDENCE
------------------------------------------------------------

Individual market participants possess internal memory.

Past success and failure influence future decision making.

Different agents may develop persistent behavioral tendencies based on personal historical outcomes.

This creates individual path dependence.

------------------------------------------------------------
LIMITATION OF PERFECT MODELING
------------------------------------------------------------

It is not possible to fully model every agent’s internal utility function.

Agents differ according to:

Risk tolerance

Investment horizon

Personal conviction

 Behavioral bias

Institutional mandate constraints

Private historical experience

A complete simulation of all market participants is intractable.

------------------------------------------------------------
THEORETICAL OPTIMAL STATE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define:

C*(t)

as the theoretically world consistent capital allocation state.

This does not imply real market participants choose this allocation.

It serves as a reference state.

------------------------------------------------------------
PRACTICAL OBJECTIVE
------------------------------------------------------------

Observed market allocation:

Ĉ(t)

may diverge from theoretical allocation:

C*(t)

The objective of 5DMarkets is not perfect market simulation.

The objective is identification of sufficiently large divergence between world consistent allocation and observed market allocation.

Such divergence creates opportunity for capital deployment capable of outperforming consensus.

------------------------------------------------------------
CORE PRINCIPLE
------------------------------------------------------------

The goal is not to model every market participant.

The goal is to identify where collective market computation is sufficiently incorrect that strategic capital deployment can outperform the market.
