# Theoretical Framework

This work proposes a new foundational interpretation of financial markets.

Traditional quantitative finance assumes that financial systems are stochastic systems whose future behavior can only be modeled probabilistically through historical price observations.

We propose a fundamentally different interpretation.

Financial markets should be understood as distributed computational systems whose observable price behavior emerges from hidden collective information processing and resulting capital redistribution.

------------------------------------------------------------
FUNDAMENTAL MARKET COMPUTATION THEOREM
------------------------------------------------------------

Financial markets consist of heterogeneous interacting agents which continuously process new information and collectively compute future capital allocation decisions.

Capital movement through the financial system represents the physical manifestation of this distributed computation.

Observed price behavior emerges secondarily as a delayed consequence of underlying capital redistribution.

Formally:

Information Input

→

Distributed Computation

→

Capital Redistribution

→

Observable Price Formation

------------------------------------------------------------
PRINCIPLE 1
------------------------------------------------------------

Markets are computational systems.

At every moment, institutions, algorithmic systems, retail investors, passive funds, and market participants continuously process incoming information.

Each participant computes expected future asset attractiveness.

The collective market continuously computes future capital allocation decisions.

------------------------------------------------------------
PRINCIPLE 2
------------------------------------------------------------

Capital represents the conserved transport quantity of financial systems.

Define total global deployable capital:

Σ C_i(t) + C_idle(t) = K_global

Capital does not disappear.

Capital continuously changes allocation state throughout the financial ecosystem.

------------------------------------------------------------
PRINCIPLE 3
------------------------------------------------------------

Price is not the fundamental market variable.

Observed price emerges from hidden capital concentration.

Define:

P_i(t) = f(C_i(t),L_i(t))

where

C_i(t) = capital concentration

L_i(t) = liquidity state

Price therefore represents an observable consequence rather than a primary system variable.

------------------------------------------------------------
PRINCIPLE 4
------------------------------------------------------------

Future market evolution is deterministic conditional on complete system state.

Apparent randomness reflects incomplete observation of hidden system variables.

Unexpected events enter the system through exogenous forcing functions.

Future state evolves according to:

dΨ/dt = F(Ψ_t,H_t,D_t,E_t,A_t^alg)

where future system state emerges from deterministic state evolution.

------------------------------------------------------------
PRINCIPLE 5
------------------------------------------------------------

Markets continuously generate present truth state.

The market does not seek hidden truth.

The market itself continuously constructs truth through collective computation and resulting capital movement.

At every moment:

Ψ(t) = Ψ*(t)

where current realized state represents true present market configuration.

------------------------------------------------------------
CENTRAL CLAIM
------------------------------------------------------------

Financial markets should not be modeled primarily as stochastic price systems.

Financial markets should be modeled as distributed computational systems whose collective information processing governs capital transport dynamics and produces observable price formation as a delayed secondary effect.
