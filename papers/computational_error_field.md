# Computational Error Field

Financial markets do not compute objective optimal capital allocation perfectly.

Given identical information, heterogeneous market participants may compute different allocation decisions.

This creates distributed computational error.

------------------------------------------------------------
OPTIMAL COMPUTATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Define:

C*(t)

where

C*(t)

represents optimal capital allocation computation under perfect information and fully rational decision making.

------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVED COMPUTATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Define:

Ĉ(t)

where

Ĉ(t)

represents actual market computation performed by real market participants.

Observed computation may differ due to:

Incomplete information

Institutional delay

Behavioral bias

Algorithmic limitations

Asymmetric information access

------------------------------------------------------------
COMPUTATIONAL ERROR FIELD
------------------------------------------------------------

Define:

ε_c(t)

=

|C*(t) - Ĉ(t)|

where

ε_c(t)

represents divergence between optimal and observed market computation.

------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVER VARIABILITY
------------------------------------------------------------

Different market participants may compute different decisions given identical information.

This parallels inter observer and intra observer variability observed in physical measurement systems.

Market inefficiency emerges from distributed computational disagreement.

------------------------------------------------------------
CORE PRINCIPLE
------------------------------------------------------------

Markets do not compute perfectly.

Markets continuously approximate optimal capital allocation under distributed computational error.
