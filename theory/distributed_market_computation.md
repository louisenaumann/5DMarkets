# Distributed Market Computation Theory

Financial markets are not fundamentally price systems.

Financial markets are not fundamentally capital transport systems.

Financial markets are distributed computational systems.

Capital movement and price formation emerge as physical manifestations of collective computation.

------------------------------------------------------------
COMPUTATIONAL MARKET PRINCIPLE
------------------------------------------------------------

Markets continuously receive new information.

Market participants independently process information.

Each participant computes expected future asset attractiveness.

Collective computation determines future capital allocation.

Capital movement generates observable price changes.

------------------------------------------------------------
COMPUTATIONAL FLOW
------------------------------------------------------------

Information

↓

Distributed Computation

↓

Capital Movement

↓

Price Formation

------------------------------------------------------------

C(t)

------------------------------------------------------------
CAPITAL MOVEMENT EMERGENCE
------------------------------------------------------------

Capital movement emerges from computation.

Define:

dC_i/dt = F(C(t))

where

dC_i/dt

represents change in capital allocation.

Capital movement reflects aggregate market computation.

------------------------------------------------------------
CORE PRINCIPLE
------------------------------------------------------------

Markets behave as decentralized computational systems.

All participants continuously process information.

The collective system computes future capital allocation.

Price emerges as a delayed observable consequence of distributed market computation.
