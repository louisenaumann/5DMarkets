# Capital Potential Field

Capital movement through financial markets is not random.

Capital moves according to continuously changing asset attractiveness states.

We define this as the Capital Potential Field.

------------------------------------------------------------
GLOBAL CAPITAL CONSERVATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Total capital is conserved across the global financial ecosystem.

Define:

Σ C_i(t) + C_idle(t) = K_global

where

C_i(t) = capital allocated to asset i

C_idle(t) = temporarily inactive capital

K_global = total deployable capital

Capital cannot disappear.

It changes allocation state.

------------------------------------------------------------
CAPITAL POTENTIAL FUNCTION
------------------------------------------------------------

Each asset possesses attractiveness potential.

Define:

U_i(t)

where

U_i(t)

represents capital attractiveness state of asset i.

Higher potential attracts capital.

------------------------------------------------------------
CAPITAL FLOW EQUATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Capital movement between assets is driven by potential differences.

Define:

F_ij(t)

=

η_ij (U_j(t) - U_i(t))

where

F_ij(t) = capital flow from asset i to asset j

η_ij = migration coefficient

U_i(t) = asset attractiveness potential

------------------------------------------------------------
INTERPRETATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Capital does not move randomly.

Capital moves toward regions of higher expected future utility.

Financial markets behave as dynamic capital flow systems governed by changing potential gradients.
