# Capital Allocation Field

The fundamental state variable in financial markets is not price.

The fundamental state variable is capital allocation.

We define the hidden market system as a continuously evolving capital allocation field.

------------------------------------------------------------
GLOBAL CAPITAL ALLOCATION FIELD
------------------------------------------------------------

Define:

Λ_t

=

{C_1(t),C_2(t),...,C_n(t)}

where

C_i(t)

=

capital allocated to asset i at time t

The system state consists of capital distribution across all assets.

------------------------------------------------------------
INTERPRETATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Assets do not primarily represent prices.

Assets represent containers for capital concentration.

Price is a secondary observable generated by capital concentration.

------------------------------------------------------------
PRICE EMERGENCE
------------------------------------------------------------

Observed asset price emerges as a function of capital allocation.

Define:

P_i(t)

=

f(C_i(t))

where

P_i(t)

=

observed market price

C_i(t)

=

capital concentration within asset i

------------------------------------------------------------
MARKET DYNAMICS
------------------------------------------------------------

The market evolves through redistribution of capital.

Capital entering an asset increases price pressure.

Capital leaving an asset decreases price pressure.

Future prediction therefore requires forecasting future capital movement rather than simple price movement.

------------------------------------------------------------
CORE PRINCIPLE
------------------------------------------------------------

Markets are capital allocation systems.

Price is an emergent observable variable generated by hidden capital concentration.

The objective of the 5DMarkets framework is reconstruction and prediction of hidden capital flow dynamics.
