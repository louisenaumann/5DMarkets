# Market Continuity Equation

The fundamental conserved quantity in financial markets is not price.

The fundamental conserved quantity is capital allocation.

Total deployable capital is redistributed continuously across assets.

Capital allocated to one asset cannot simultaneously remain allocated elsewhere.

------------------------------------------------------------
CAPITAL CONSERVATION LAW
------------------------------------------------------------

Define:

C_i(t)

=

capital allocated to asset i at time t

Total system capital:

K_total

Then

Σ C_i(t)

=

K_total

Capital is conserved.

It redistributes but is not created or destroyed within the market system.

------------------------------------------------------------
MARKET CONTINUITY EQUATION
------------------------------------------------------------

The rate of capital change for asset i is defined as:

dC_i/dt

=

Σ F_ji(t)

-

Σ F_ij(t)

where

F_ij(t)

=

capital flow from asset i to asset j

------------------------------------------------------------
INTERPRETATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Capital entering asset i increases valuation pressure.

Capital leaving asset i decreases valuation pressure.

Observed price changes are consequences of underlying capital redistribution.

------------------------------------------------------------
CORE PRINCIPLE
------------------------------------------------------------

Markets are not price systems.

Markets are capital transport systems.

Price represents observable manifestation of hidden capital concentration.

The objective of 5DMarkets is therefore reconstruction and prediction of capital movement rather than simple price prediction.
