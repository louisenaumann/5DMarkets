# Falsification Framework

Scientific progress requires active attempts to disprove theory.

The objective of this phase is to identify assumptions which may invalidate the 5DMarkets framework.

------------------------------------------------------------
ASSUMPTION 1
------------------------------------------------------------

Capital is conserved.

Challenge:

Capital may leave the market system entirely.

Possible revision:

Replace fixed capital conservation with time varying active deployable capital.

------------------------------------------------------------
ASSUMPTION 2
------------------------------------------------------------

Price emerges from capital concentration.

Challenge:

Price movement depends on liquidity structure.

Possible revision:

P_i = f(C_i,L_i)

where L_i represents liquidity state.

------------------------------------------------------------
ASSUMPTION 3
------------------------------------------------------------

Markets are deterministic.

Challenge:

Unexpected exogenous events may appear without prior state observability.

Possible revision:

Treat randomness as unobserved exogenous forcing.

------------------------------------------------------------
ASSUMPTION 4
------------------------------------------------------------

Algorithmic expectation influences market evolution.

Challenge:

Aggregate algorithmic behavior is not directly observable.

Possible revision:

Infer algorithmic behavior indirectly through market microstructure.

------------------------------------------------------------
ASSUMPTION 5
------------------------------------------------------------

Capital allocation behaves rationally.

Challenge:

Social feedback loops and irrational behavior may override efficient capital movement.

Possible revision:

Include behavioral forcing term representing irrational market behavior.
